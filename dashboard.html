About AdLens

AdLens turns your raw Amazon Sponsored Products report into a clear picture of where your ad budget is actually going — and which campaigns, search terms and products are worth scaling, watching, or cutting.

What it does

  • Reads your Sponsored Products report and automatically calculates ACOS, ROAS, CTR and CVR for every campaign.
  • Matches each Advertised ASIN to its real product title automatically, so you're never staring at a bare ASIN code.
  • Breaks down search terms separately, so you can spot wasted spend on irrelevant keywords.
  • Flags each campaign as Scale, Maintain, Reduce, or Pause based on its ROAS — so the next action is obvious, not buried in a spreadsheet.

Why it's worth using

  • No manual VLOOKUPs or pivot tables — upload and see the breakdown in seconds.
  • Your last report is saved automatically, so you can pick up where you left off without re-uploading.
  • Free to use, with no ads and no data sold to third parties.

How to use AdLens

1. Get your reports from Amazon Ads

In Amazon Advertising Console, go to Measurement & Reporting → create a Sponsored Products "Advertised product" report (and optionally a Search Term report) for the date range you want, then download it as .xlsx.

2. Upload here

Use the "Products Report" box for your campaign/advertised-product file, and the "Keywords Report" box for your search term file if you have one. The search term file is optional — campaign-level analysis works without it.

3. Click Analyze

The dashboard builds itself in a few seconds. Product titles for each ASIN are fetched automatically in the background — if a few don't resolve, use the "Retry failed titles" button that appears.

4. Read the breakdown

Use the date range buttons to zoom into a specific week or day. Switch between the Campaigns, Search Terms and Products tabs to drill into each level. Filter by status, ROAS or ACOS to quickly find campaigns that need attention.

5. Download or save

Download Report exports everything to an Excel file. Your analysis is also saved automatically to your account, so it's waiting for you next time on the Launchpad.
